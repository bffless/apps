# Companion blog post is derived from the final cut, not the raw recording

The blog-post feature builds a **companion** to the produced video: same content and
coverage as the [[Final cut]], in blog format. It is written from the **final narration
script** (`videoScript(scenes)`) and the kept scenes — **not** from the original 45-min
rambling transcript.

We considered building a richer standalone article off the untouched recording (it could
cover material the director cut), and a hybrid. We chose the companion framing because the
intent is "a readable version of the video I just published," and because every ingredient
the feature needs — final script, title, summary, synopsis, scene titles, contact sheets —
already exists at the point Build finishes. A standalone-from-raw blog would be a different,
heavier product needing the untouched transcript and a separate image pass over footage the
director deliberately discarded.

Reversing this (switching the source to the raw recording) would change the inputs, the
prompt, and the image-selection scope — i.e. most of the feature — so it's recorded here.
